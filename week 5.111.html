The scanner is a peripheral device that inserts a certain type of graphic information into a computer. The scanner is used to capture (digitally display) a document or image that can then be used on computers. As a result of the scanner's work, files are captured with the captured objects - photographic pictures, documents, or real 3D objects. The basic principle of the scanner is similar to the fax system, both of which integrate a transmitter and receiver into a single device. However, the fax machine (because its purpose is to transmit and receive images) connects to another fax machine elsewhere, one transmitting and the other accepting the image.

Cutting materials

After creating the material or the model there are machines which are used to crop them,depends on their size.Laser cutting is a technology that uses a laser to cut materials, and is typically used for industrial manufacturing applications, but is also starting to be used by schools, small businesses, and hobbyists. Laser cutting works by directing the output of a high-power laser most commonly through optics. The laser optics and CNC (computer numerical control) are used to direct the material or the laser beam generated. A typical commercial laser for cutting materials involved a motion control system to follow a CNC or G-code of the pattern to be cut onto the material. The focused laser beam is directed at the material, which then either melts, burns, vaporizes away, or is blown away by a jet of gas,leaving an edge with a high-quality surface finish. Industrial laser cutters are used to cut flat-sheet material as well as structural and piping materials.

Laser cutting is a method of cutting sheet material without any limitations in shape and contours. It is a quick and efficient tool for laser cutting of sheet material. In the correct choice of this method, depending on the specificity of the cut, the size of the batches of the products, laser cutting is the fastest and most advantageous method of cutting sheet material.

This is the machine of cutting the materials,the Laser Cutter.The materials which we can use can be different,starting from a soft textile until to diffrent types of leather.

How it works?In this part we was shortly introduced,how the Laser Cutter works and the features of it.Generation of the laser beam involves stimulating a lasing material by electrical discharges or lamps within a closed container. As the lasing material is stimulated, the beam is reflected internally by means of a partial mirror, until it achieves sufficient energy to escape as a stream of monochromatic coherent light. Mirrors or fiber optics are typically used to direct the coherent light to a lens, which focuses the light at the work zone.

Advantages of laser cutting over mechanical cutting include easier workholding and reduced contamination of workpiece (since there is no cutting edge which can become contaminated by the material or contaminate the material). Precision may be better, since the laser beam does not wear during the process. There is also a reduced chance of warping the material that is being cut, as laser systems have a small heat-affected zone. Some materials are also very difficult or impossible to cut by more traditional means.
