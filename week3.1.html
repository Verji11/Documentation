What is CAD Modelling?

Computer-aided design (CAD), explicitly referred to as software and hardware tools for geometric modeling, enabling the design and testing of industrial products in the virtual environment (computer and computer simulation) and the tools required for their production. CAD systems have replaced the drawing board as well as many routine tasks in designing documentation for mechanical, electrical or electronic products. They are used in industries such as precision machine building, machine building, automotive, aerospace, shipbuilding, dentistry, architecture and civil engineering. According to their purpose, CAD systems can allow both modeling of 2D and 3D modeling.

3D CAD Modeling

The creation of 3D CAD models (Computer-Aided Design) of objects, captured by 3D laser scanner, represents the transformation process of the three-dimensional point clouds into a virtual reality which is intended to solve specific tasks concerning the reconstruction, simulation and animation of the detected environment and its objects. 3D CAD-modeling based on 3D laser scanning is applicable in many business areas, incl. construction, automotive industry, shipbuilding and ship repair industry, aerospace, ports and airports, power plants, factories, oil refineries, architecture, medicine, etc. The object-oriented 3D CAD modeling is used to create digital simulation of infrastructures, buildings, plants and installations for quality improvement and quality assurance of the design and production process.
